Webb photographs complexity without letting it collapse into noise. A single frame
might carry three separate events, stitched together by a shadow and the accident of
where he stood.

![Midday, hard light doing the composing](3.jpg 'wide')

He works the border and the Caribbean, where the sun is unforgiving and the colour is
loud, and he waits — sometimes for hours — for the layers to line up.

## Standing in the heat

The pictures feel effortless and are the opposite. Each one is a wager that the whole
chaotic scene will resolve, for a fraction of a second, into order.

> Photography is a matter of standing in the right place, long enough.
