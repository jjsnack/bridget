Erwitt taught a generation that a serious photograph could also be a funny one. He
worked in black and white, at ankle height as often as not, and waited for the world
to make its own joke inside the frame.

![Two figures and the space between them](1.jpg 'wide')

Nothing is staged and everything is composed. The gag is never the point — the point
is that the gag and the geometry arrive at the same instant, and he was there for it.

## The long wait

His best pictures look like luck and are nothing of the sort. He stood still until the
street composed itself, then took the one frame it owed him.

> The whole point of taking pictures is so that you don't have to explain things with words.
